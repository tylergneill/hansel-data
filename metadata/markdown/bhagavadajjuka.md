# Work Description

Bhagavadajjukam (“The Holy Man and the Harlot”) is a one-act Sanskrit farce (prahasana), traditionally associated with Bodhāyana and preserved in a South Indian manuscript tradition, perhaps composed in the 6th or 7th century CE. It is a mixed Sanskrit–Prakrit drama in which an ascetic, his pupil, and a courtesan (ajjukā) become entangled in a comic confusion of souls and bodies. The satire targets notions of religious discipline, intellectual discourse, and bodily identity.

In the Straube–Steiner edition, the work is presented as one whose transmitted form required substantial editorial intervention, building on the important edition of Achan (1925) but going further through comparison with other editions (Banerji-Śāstrī, Prabhākaraśāstrī) and through numerous normalizations of orthography, punctuation, and Prakrit dialectical form.
